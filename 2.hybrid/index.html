Overview

We aim to create hybrid images, which are composed of 2 different images, one for high frequency and one for low. Up close, high frequency images dominate, but from far away the low does instead.

Approach

The input is 2 images, one for low frequency and one for high frequency. I applied Gaussian filter to first and second image, and subtracted the second image from the original second image. The width and height of the Gaussian filter is 1/20 of the images' height, and the sigma is 1/150 of the images' height.

Bells and Whistles

I added color to my output. However, I only used color on the low-frequency component. Using color on high-frequency components mostly made no difference, but on some parts of images it created extremely brightly colored artifacts, which made the outputs look worse, so I ended up taking the greyscale of the high frequency images.
